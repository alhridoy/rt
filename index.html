”When will the peak of the pandemic hit? When will it be over?”¶

Those are unarguably among the most asked questions during the ongoing coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) crisis.The answer lies in a simple but powerful metric known as Basic Reproduction number or 𝑅.The 𝑅 can be estimated at the beginning of an epidemic and its constant when no immunity intervention measures are implemented, When an epidemic progresses a more practical and an effective version of 𝑅, the Rt can be estimated, which refers to the number of susceptible people in a population who can be infected by an infected individual at any given time. Although the basic reproduction number 𝑅 is useful for understanding the transmissibility of a disease it assumes that the epidemic first occurs in a fully susceptible population, hence, it is important to evaluate time-dependent variations in the transmission potential of infectious diseases (Nishiura & Chowell, 2009)

In any epidemic, 𝑅𝑡 is the measure known as the effective reproduction number. It's the number of people who become infected per infectious person at time 𝑡 . The most well-known version of this number is the basic reproduction number: 𝑅0 when 𝑡=0 . However, 𝑅0 is a single measure that does not adapt with changes in behavior and restrictions.

As a pandemic evolves, increasing restrictions (or potential releasing of restrictions) changes 𝑅𝑡 . Knowing the current 𝑅𝑡 is essential. When 𝑅≫1 , the pandemic will spread through a large part of the population. If 𝑅𝑡<1 , the pandemic will slow quickly before it has a chance to infect many people. The lower the 𝑅𝑡 : the more manageable the situation. In general, any 𝑅𝑡<1 means things are under control, the virus is likely to die out since.

The value of 𝑅𝑡 helps us in two ways. (1) It helps us understand how effective our measures have been controlling an outbreak and (2) it gives us vital information about whether we should increase or reduce restrictions based on our competing goals of economic prosperity and human safety. Well-respected epidemiologists argue that tracking 𝑅𝑡 is the only way to manage through this crisis.

Situation becomes manageable when Rt becomes long way below cruicial thresold 1.if 𝑅≫1 it indicates exponential growth of cases ,and cases will continue to grow.

Since there is no proven vaccine developed for COVID-19 yet and absent an indefinite lockdown in Bangladesh due to its high socioeconomic cost it will be very difficult for Bangladesh to get Rt a long way below 1. Moreover, there will be always new imported cases, local outbreak, hence, a careful calibration of measures is needed, where Rt will shed some light on the COVID-19 situation in Bangladesh.

As the COVID-19 pandemic already continuing in Bangladesh, despite no nation-wide lockdown in place, schools, college, universities remained close till now, it is important to watch daily Rt for Bangladesh to set up comprehensive strategies by policy-makers.

Second Model¶

According to a recent paper "Practical considerations for measuring the effective reproductive number, Rt"(June,2020) Gostic et al.(2020)[1] reported that Adapted Bettencourt and Ribeiro (2008)[2] methods(Which is our first method), may be biased if the underlying structural assumptions are not met, since it depends on many parameters.The author recommend the approach of Cori et al. (2013).In addition the method of Cori et al. (2013) has been using by Imperial college London to estimates Rt worldwide(https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-020-2405-7 ) , also by (https://epiforecasts.io/covid ).

Addressing the limitation of our first model, the author also considered to use the methods of Cori et al .(2013) to estimate daily reproduction number for COVID-19 in Bangladesh.

In addition, Rt estimation for Bangladesh (https://epiforecasts.io/covid/posts/national/bangladesh/) do not provide daily Rt estimation,they are at least 10 days behind, thus given the urgency of COVID-19 situation in Bangladesh , author is inspired to estimate daily real-time Rt for COVID-19 in Bangladesh.

Methods¶

We used the inferred number of cases to estimate the reproduction number on each day using the EpiEstim R package[1] and later extened by Thompson et al [2] . This uses a combination of the serial interval distribution and the number of observed cases to estimate the reproduction number at each time point [2,3], which were then smoothed using a 7-day time window. We assumed that the serial interval was uncertain with a mean of 3.96 days and a standard deviation of 4.75 days according to recent findings bu Du et al[4] . We used a common prior for the reproduction number with mean 2.6 and a standard deviation of 2 (inflated from 0.5 found in the reference)[5]

Limitation¶

The model face limitations associated with data uncertainties such as differences in testing, inconsistent, delayed reporting, and low number cases, and high variability. The results are impacted by testing rate, increases and decreases in testing rate will increase and decrease reproduction number estimates, respectively.

While author made best effort to accurately describe Rt, nothing can do that perfectly. Please calibrate with other sources, like https://epiforecasts.io/covid/posts/national/bangladesh/, as well.

Test adjusted Rt estimation¶

To address the of limitation of testing effort on true case, the author also used two techniques to estimate true actual case and Rt.

1.Generalized Methods: Equation 1: y=mxq^k+ϵ

where,

y is the true cases x is the confirmed cases/observed cases m is an unknown constant multiplier of x

q is the test positivity rate k is an unknown constant exponent of q, between zero and one. ϵ is a random error term of unknown distribution

Limitation: Taking test positivity rates as a proxy for estimate true cases may lead to better estimates of growth and of effective reproduction number. But it can’t work miracles - we don’t know how to calibrate it so it’s not going to be good for estimating the absolute value. And we still can’t get reliable estimates of growth or effective reproduction number when the data just aren’t there to support it.
